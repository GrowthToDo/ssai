## TL;DR

NurseGrid is a nurse-facing scheduling app with good mobile UX but limited compliance and management features. For critical access hospitals, the gaps matter: no proactive FLSA overtime tracking, no CMS-compliant audit trail, and a manager app that reviewers describe as non-functional. The main alternatives are TCP Scheduling Software for a self-serve platform with more management features, and SimpleScheduleAI for a managed service covering full CAH compliance requirements.

## What NurseGrid Is

NurseGrid is a scheduling tool built primarily for nurses. Its strongest feature is the nurse-facing mobile experience: nurses can view their schedules, manage availability, and request shift trades through a clean mobile app. It is lighter-weight than enterprise scheduling platforms and faster to get nurses using.

For clinics with simple scheduling needs and no inpatient CMS requirements, NurseGrid works. For critical access hospitals, the compliance gaps make it insufficient as a primary scheduling system.

## Why CAHs Look for NurseGrid Alternatives

Based on G2 and Capterra reviews, these are the issues that drive NurseGrid users to evaluate alternatives:

**App crashes and force-close issues.** This is the most frequently cited problem. Multiple reviewers describe the app crashing mid-shift entry or during schedule management. For a nurse manager who relies on the scheduling app for real-time coverage decisions, reliability is non-negotiable.

**Manager app no longer functions on mobile.** Reviewers specifically describe the manager-facing app as broken or severely limited. The nurse-facing experience works; the manager-facing experience does not. For a nurse manager who needs to check and adjust schedules from the floor, this is a disqualifying limitation.

**Poor Android compatibility.** NurseGrid's mobile experience is optimized for iOS. Android users report significant functionality gaps and reliability problems.

**No FLSA overtime tracking.** NurseGrid does not track cumulative weekly hours per nurse or flag overtime risk during schedule building. For a critical access hospital, [FLSA](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/flsa) overtime compliance is a core scheduling requirement. Without proactive overtime visibility, overtime surprises surface at payroll rather than being prevented during scheduling.

**Messaging inbox management issues.** Reviewers report that the internal messaging feature has no way to clear the inbox, creating clutter that makes the communication feature less useful over time.

**No CMS audit trail.** NurseGrid does not produce the change documentation required for [CMS §485.635](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/certification-compliance/critical-access-hospitals) survey response. For a CAH, this means maintaining a separate documentation process for all schedule changes.

### TCP Scheduling Software (formerly Aladtec)

TCP is the most widely deployed scheduling platform in critical access hospital settings. It covers the management and compliance requirements that NurseGrid misses: certification tracking, overtime visibility (manual), and basic audit trail capability. The trade-off is a more complex platform requiring 2 to 4 weeks of setup and ongoing operation by the nurse manager.

If you are switching from NurseGrid because of reliability and compliance gaps, TCP covers those gaps while maintaining a self-serve model. The nurse-facing mobile experience is more limited than NurseGrid, which may frustrate staff who have been using NurseGrid's mobile app.

### ShiftWizard

ShiftWizard has a nurse-facing app and is designed specifically for hospital scheduling. It covers more compliance features than NurseGrid and has a more modern interface than TCP. Known weaknesses: mobile app has limited department visibility for managers, shift swap reliability issues, and weak onboarding support.

For a CAH switching from NurseGrid specifically because of the manager app problems, ShiftWizard's own mobile manager experience is worth scrutinizing before committing.

The honest trade-off in the other direction from NurseGrid: SimpleScheduleAI does not have a nurse-facing mobile app. If nurse self-service features are a significant reason your facility uses NurseGrid, that function does not transfer to SimpleScheduleAI. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What are the biggest problems with NurseGrid for hospital use?**

Based on G2 and Capterra reviews: the manager-facing app is non-functional, the iOS app crashes during shift entry and management, Android compatibility is poor, there is no FLSA overtime tracking, and there is no CMS-compliant audit trail. NurseGrid works well as a nurse-facing communication tool but is not designed as a primary hospital scheduling system.

**Q: Is NurseGrid free?**

NurseGrid has a free tier for individual nurses viewing their own schedules. The manager and facility management features are in paid tiers. The compliance and management features required for CAH operations are not in the free tier.

**Q: Can I use NurseGrid for staff communication and switch to a different tool for scheduling management?**

Yes. This hybrid approach makes sense for facilities that value NurseGrid's nurse-facing mobile features. Nurses continue using NurseGrid to view schedules; the manager uses a different system for building schedules, tracking overtime, and maintaining compliance documentation. The coordination overhead is manageable if the two systems have clear, separate functions.

**Q: How does NurseGrid handle FLSA compliance?**

It does not, beyond basic hours display. NurseGrid shows scheduled hours but does not proactively calculate overtime risk across the workweek or flag nurses approaching the 40-hour threshold during schedule building. For CAH nurse managers who need to prevent overtime rather than just report it, a compliance-capable platform or managed service is required.

## Key Takeaways

- NurseGrid's strength is nurse-facing mobile features; its gaps are compliance, management, and reliability features required for inpatient hospital scheduling
- Critical access hospitals need proactive FLSA overtime tracking, CMS §485.635 audit trail, and certification-unit matching that NurseGrid does not provide
- The main alternatives are TCP (more management features, self-serve), ShiftWizard (nurse-specific, more modern, self-serve), and SimpleScheduleAI (managed service, full compliance coverage, no nurse app)
- A hybrid approach using NurseGrid for nurse communication and a managed service for scheduling management is a viable option for CAHs that value both
- The right alternative depends on whether compliance coverage or nurse-facing features is the higher priority
