## TL;DR

TCP Scheduling Software, formerly known as Aladtec, is a self-serve scheduling platform used by EMS, fire, and healthcare organizations. The top alternatives for critical access hospitals are ShiftWizard (self-serve, nurse-specific), SimpleScheduleAI (managed service, no configuration required), and NurseGrid (lightweight, nurse-facing). The right choice depends on whether your nurse manager wants to operate a scheduling platform or have the scheduling work done for her.

## What Is TCP Scheduling Software?

TCP Scheduling Software is the product previously known as Aladtec, which TCP acquired in 2021. The platform continues to operate under the Aladtec interface for most customers, though branding has shifted to TCP in newer documentation and sales materials. If you're searching for "Aladtec alternatives," you're looking at the same product.

TCP is a self-serve scheduling platform: your nurse manager logs in, builds schedules, manages availability, and handles callout coverage through the platform's interface. TCP provides the structure; your team provides the labor to run it.

For organizations with a technically comfortable scheduler and predictable staffing patterns, TCP works. The problems show up at smaller hospitals, particularly critical access hospitals, where the nurse manager has no scheduling coordinator and no IT support.

## Why People Look for TCP Scheduling Software Alternatives

The G2 and Capterra reviews for TCP (Aladtec) follow a consistent pattern. These are the complaints that appear most frequently:

**"Too many clicks to do anything."** Multiple reviewers describe the scheduling workflow as requiring an excessive number of steps for routine tasks. One reviewer noted that schedules must be built one day at a time, with no bulk assignment option. For a nurse manager building a 4-week schedule for 15 nurses, this adds up fast.

**Steep learning curve.** New users consistently report a significant time investment before the platform feels manageable. For a nurse manager who is also responsible for clinical supervision, floor coverage, and staff development, weeks of learning a new scheduling tool is not a minor ask.

**Declining support quality post-acquisition.** This is the most specific and concerning pattern. Multiple G2 reviewers explicitly mention that customer support quality declined noticeably after the TCP acquisition. Users who had positive relationships with Aladtec's support team report a different experience under TCP ownership.

**Can't hide unpublished schedules from staff.** A practical workflow problem: nurse managers want to build a draft schedule, review it, and publish when ready. TCP apparently shows draft schedules to staff before the manager has finalized them, which creates confusion and premature schedule requests.

**Complex initial configuration.** Setting up the platform for a new facility requires entering all staff records, certification data, unit rules, and availability constraints. Without IT support or dedicated scheduling staff, this setup period can run 2 to 4 weeks before the first operational schedule.

## What to Look for in a TCP Alternative

Before evaluating alternatives, clarify what problem you're actually trying to solve:

**If the problem is the interface:** You want a different self-serve platform with a better user experience. ShiftWizard and NurseGrid are worth evaluating.

**If the problem is the workload:** The issue isn't the tool, it's that your nurse manager is spending 8 to 12 hours per week on scheduling tasks regardless of which platform she uses. A managed service addresses this differently than a platform swap.

**If the problem is compliance:** You need healthcare-specific features, proactive [FLSA](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/flsa) overtime tracking, CMS audit trail documentation, certification-unit matching. Confirm any alternative covers these explicitly, not generically.

**If the problem is implementation speed:** You need to be operational in days, not weeks. Managed services onboard significantly faster than self-serve platforms.

### ShiftWizard

ShiftWizard is a healthcare-specific scheduling platform designed by nurses and used by 500+ hospitals. It covers the core healthcare scheduling requirements: certification tracking, overtime visibility, and shift management. The interface is more modern than TCP.

Where ShiftWizard falls short according to G2 and Capterra reviews: the mobile app lacks desktop feature parity (managers can't view department schedules or access full date ranges on mobile), shift swapping is unreliable, and onboarding support is weak. The platform is self-serve, so setup and ongoing operation still fall to your nurse manager.

### SimpleScheduleAI

SimpleScheduleAI is a managed [healthcare staff scheduling](/healthcare-staff-scheduling) service, not a platform. A scheduling specialist handles configuration, roster maintenance, and weekly schedule generation. The nurse manager reviews and approves draft schedules.

Setup takes 3 to 5 business days via Excel roster upload. No IT integration, no configuration work, no learning period. Texas compliance is built in as default: [FLSA](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/flsa) overtime rules, [Texas Labor Code](https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/LA/htm/LA.62.htm) provisions, and [CMS CoP §485.635](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/certification-compliance/critical-access-hospitals) documentation.

One honest limitation: SimpleScheduleAI does not have a staff-facing mobile app. Nurses do not interact with the scheduling system directly. If staff self-service scheduling is a priority, a hybrid approach, generic communication tool for nurse-facing features plus managed service for scheduling management, may be worth considering.

### NurseGrid

NurseGrid is a nurse-facing scheduling tool with a mobile app. It is lighter-weight than TCP and easier to learn. The trade-off: NurseGrid does not include proactive overtime tracking or CMS-compliant audit trail documentation. For a critical access hospital with [CMS Conditions of Participation](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/certification-compliance/critical-access-hospitals) requirements, NurseGrid's compliance features are insufficient as a primary scheduling system.

## How SimpleScheduleAI Compares to TCP

The fundamental difference is not features, it is operating model.

TCP is software your nurse manager runs. She enters the data, builds the schedules, manages exceptions, and handles callout coverage through the platform. TCP automates parts of the process, but she is still the operator.

SimpleScheduleAI is a service that runs scheduling for her. A specialist generates draft schedules, manages the callout shortlist, tracks overtime proactively, and maintains the audit trail. The nurse manager's role is review and approval.

The time difference is significant. A well-run TCP implementation reduces scheduling from 8 to 12 hours per week to 4 to 6 hours. A managed service reduces it to 1 to 2 hours.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is TCP Scheduling Software the same as Aladtec?**

Yes. Aladtec was acquired by TCP in 2021. The product continues to operate under the Aladtec interface for most existing customers, but new sales and documentation use the TCP Scheduling Software brand. If you have Aladtec, you have TCP. If you're evaluating "Aladtec alternatives," you're evaluating TCP alternatives.

**Q: What are the biggest complaints about TCP (Aladtec) from current users?**

Based on G2 and Capterra reviews: too many clicks for routine scheduling tasks, steep learning curve for new users, declining customer support quality since the TCP acquisition, and complex initial setup. The platform works, but the operational burden it places on the scheduler is significant at small facilities without dedicated scheduling staff.

**Q: How long does it take to switch from TCP to a different scheduling tool?**

Switching to another self-serve platform (ShiftWizard, NurseGrid) takes 2 to 4 weeks for setup plus a transition period. Switching to a managed service takes 3 to 5 days for onboarding. The managed service approach is faster because the service team handles configuration rather than your nurse manager.

**Q: Do TCP alternatives integrate with EHR systems?**

Enterprise platforms (UKG, API Healthcare) offer EHR integration. Most mid-market alternatives, including ShiftWizard and SimpleScheduleAI, operate independently of the EHR. For critical access hospitals, standalone scheduling without EHR integration is typically the more practical option, it avoids IT complexity while still covering scheduling and compliance requirements.

## Key Takeaways

- TCP Scheduling Software (formerly Aladtec) is the dominant self-serve scheduling platform for small healthcare organizations, but common complaints include excessive clicks, steep learning curve, and post-acquisition support decline
- The main alternatives for critical access hospitals are ShiftWizard (self-serve, healthcare-specific) and SimpleScheduleAI (managed service, no configuration)
- The core question is not which platform has better features, it is whether your nurse manager wants to operate a scheduling platform or have the scheduling work done for her
- Managed services onboard in days vs. weeks for self-serve platforms, which matters when scheduling burden is acute
- Neither model is universally better, choose based on how much scheduling time your facility can afford to spend
