## TL;DR

For a critical access hospital, the best alternative to TCP Scheduling Software (formerly Aladtec) depends on one question: do you want a better tool to build schedules yourself, or do you want the scheduling work done for you? For better self-serve tools, ShiftWizard is the strongest CAH-appropriate option. For structural burden reduction where a specialist builds the schedule and the nurse manager approves, SimpleScheduleAI is the managed service alternative.

## Why CAHs Look for TCP Alternatives

TCP Scheduling Software, the platform previously known as Aladtec, is a widely used self-serve scheduling tool in healthcare and EMS settings. For critical access hospitals specifically, TCP works when a nurse manager has time to configure and operate it. The problems arise when that assumption does not hold.

At a typical 25-bed CAH, the nurse manager is responsible for clinical supervision, staff development, regulatory compliance, and scheduling. There is rarely a dedicated scheduling coordinator. The IT support model is minimal or nonexistent. When TCP becomes another system to maintain on top of everything else, the promised efficiency gains diminish.

The specific TCP complaints that drive CAH nurse managers to look for alternatives:

**Configuration burden.** TCP requires someone to enter all nurses, certifications, unit assignments, and scheduling rules before the first schedule is built. At a CAH without IT support, this falls to the nurse manager over several weeks.

**Ongoing operation.** Even after setup, TCP requires the nurse manager to build each schedule in the platform, manage availability requests, and handle callout coverage through the interface. It reduces scheduling time compared to spreadsheets, but it does not eliminate the scheduling function.

**Support quality.** Multiple G2 and Capterra reviewers specifically mention that customer support quality declined after the TCP acquisition of Aladtec. Organizations that had positive support relationships under Aladtec report a different experience under TCP ownership.

**Too many clicks.** Reviewers consistently describe routine scheduling actions as requiring excessive steps. Scheduling one day at a time with no bulk options is a recurring complaint.

## What CAHs Need That TCP Covers (and What It Misses)

TCP covers the basic requirements for CAH scheduling: shift assignment, availability tracking, overtime visibility (manual), and basic credential fields. It is healthcare-aware in a way that generic scheduling tools are not.

What TCP does not cover well:

- Proactive overtime flagging before the schedule publishes (TCP shows current hours; the manager must calculate risk manually)
- Automatic [CMS §485.635](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/certification-compliance/critical-access-hospitals) audit trail documentation (available but requires correct configuration)
- Ranked callout shortlists that apply certification, overtime, and fairness constraints automatically

For a CAH operating under [CMS Conditions of Participation](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/certification-compliance/critical-access-hospitals), these gaps create manual compliance work on top of the operational burden.

## The Best TCP Alternative for Each CAH Scenario

### Scenario 1: You want better self-serve software

If you want to keep building schedules yourself but with a better platform, **ShiftWizard** is the strongest CAH-appropriate alternative to TCP.

ShiftWizard is designed by nurses for hospital scheduling and used by 500+ facilities. It has a more modern interface than TCP and similar healthcare-specific feature coverage. Setup runs 2 to 4 weeks, comparable to TCP.

Honest limitations: ShiftWizard's mobile app has known gaps (managers cannot view full department schedules on mobile), shift swap functionality has reliability issues per reviewer reports, and onboarding support is weak. But for a nurse manager who wants direct platform control and is comfortable with self-serve tools, ShiftWizard is the strongest comparable alternative.

### Scenario 2: You want the scheduling work done for you

If the goal is structural burden reduction rather than a better tool, **SimpleScheduleAI** is the managed service alternative to TCP.

SimpleScheduleAI does not replace TCP with a different platform. It replaces the self-serve model entirely. A scheduling specialist handles configuration, roster maintenance, and weekly schedule generation. The nurse manager reviews draft schedules and approves. Setup takes 3 to 5 business days via Excel roster upload, no IT involvement, no learning period.

The operating difference:

|                   | TCP                                  | SimpleScheduleAI                  |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| Setup             | 2-4 weeks (nurse manager configures) | 3-5 days (specialist configures)  |
| Weekly scheduling | 4-6 hrs (nurse manager builds)       | 1-2 hrs (nurse manager reviews)   |
| Callout coverage  | Manager checks platform manually     | Pre-ranked shortlist delivered    |

The honest trade-off: TCP has a nurse-facing mobile app. SimpleScheduleAI does not. If nurses viewing schedules and managing availability on their phones is a priority, TCP or ShiftWizard is the right choice. If burden reduction is the priority, the managed service model addresses it more directly.

### Scenario 3: You are on a very tight budget

If budget is the primary constraint, TCP is likely the right choice over alternatives. It is a known cost, and mid-market scheduling platforms in this category run $200 to $600 per month for facilities under 50 beds. Enterprise alternatives cost significantly more. Managed service pricing is typically in a similar range to self-serve mid-market platforms but includes the implementation work.

## Evaluation Questions to Ask Any TCP Alternative

Before committing to any TCP alternative, ask these four questions:

1. **How long until my first operational schedule?** The answer should be in days (managed service) or weeks (self-serve platform). Anything measured in months is an enterprise product not designed for CAH scale.

2. **Who does the configuration work?** If the answer is "your team," understand what that means in hours for your specific facility size and scheduling complexity.

3. **What does the callout coverage workflow look like at 5am?** Walk through the specific scenario: a nurse calls out 2 hours before a shift. What does the manager do step by step? What does the platform provide vs. what does she do manually?

4. **What CMS documentation does it produce automatically?** Ask to see a sample audit trail output. "We have compliance features" is not the same as auto-logged, timestamp-stamped change documentation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is TCP Scheduling Software the same as Aladtec?**

Yes. TCP acquired Aladtec in 2021. The product continues to operate under the Aladtec interface for most existing customers, but TCP Scheduling Software is the current brand. If you are looking for Aladtec alternatives, you are looking at TCP alternatives.

**Q: What is the fastest way to switch from TCP to a new scheduling solution?**

Switching to a managed service is the fastest path: 3 to 5 days from roster upload to first draft schedule, with the service team handling configuration. Switching to another self-serve platform (ShiftWizard) runs 2 to 4 weeks and requires the nurse manager to handle setup. Confirm TCP contract terms and renewal dates before starting any transition.

**Q: Does SimpleScheduleAI replace TCP's staff-facing features?**

No. SimpleScheduleAI does not have a nurse-facing mobile app. Nurses at facilities using SimpleScheduleAI receive schedules through the facility's existing communication method (email, group text, posted schedule). If nurse-facing self-service features are important, a hybrid approach, a generic communication tool for nurse-facing features plus the managed service for scheduling management, is worth considering.

**Q: What compliance documentation does a TCP alternative need to produce for a CAH?**

At minimum: an automatically maintained log of every schedule change with timestamp, changed-by information, and the before/after state. This documentation is what CMS surveyors request when reviewing staffing records under [§485.635](https://www.cms.gov/medicare/health-safety-standards/certification-compliance/critical-access-hospitals). Confirm any alternative produces this automatically, not through manual export.

## Key Takeaways

- TCP Scheduling Software (Aladtec) is a self-serve platform that requires the nurse manager to configure, build, and maintain schedules — the burden is lower than spreadsheets but not eliminated
- The best self-serve TCP alternative for CAHs is ShiftWizard (modern interface, hospital-specific, comparable setup time)
- The best alternative if the goal is structural burden reduction is a managed scheduling service (specialist builds, nurse manager approves)
- TCP has a nurse-facing mobile app that SimpleScheduleAI does not — this is the primary reason to choose TCP over a managed service if staff self-service is a priority
- The right alternative depends on what problem you are actually trying to solve: better tools, or less work
